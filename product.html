How it works

Battlegrounds uses two different networking systems, as well as infrared lasers, to make live gaming possible.

A short range radio (1) wirelessly connects the playerâ€™s vest and gun together, eliminating the awkward tether found on other laser tag systems.

Each gun connects to the central server over a Wi-Fi radio (2), which provides high bandwidth and fast response times. There are no visible antennas for either radio.

Players tag each other with infrared lasers (3) which carry a data- packet identifying who shot who and with what weapon profile. Accurate up to 700ft.

Player Equipment

Each player wears a vest (1) which shows their team color and reports hits from other players. When a player is hit, multi-color LEDs flash and force feedback units vibrate. An optional head sensor (2) and electric shock force-on-force simulator can be added for more serious players.

Each player wears a silicone RFID bracelet (3). Just by touching their wrist to a device, players can interact with intelligent field props, information kiosks and the point- of-sale system.

The Playing Field

The playing field is the central experience at Battlegrounds. Where every feature and system we have developed comes together to produce a digitally-enhanced physical gaming environment.

Features include:

  • Ambient lighting and a surround audio system that automatically change based on pre-defined scenarios or in reaction to game events
  • Simulations of battlefield noises, busy city streets, or high-energy game music
  • Spatial augmented reality projectors that display objectives and game information
  • Live cameras streaming action to the lobby and bar
  • Dark rooms, exposed alleyways and obstacles for players to hide behind
  • Easy-to-change modular design that keeps the field layout fresh
